Spam Musubi

Description

The Spam Musubi is a classic snack from Hawaii.

The Spam Musubi takes inspiration from the Japanese sushi roll,
where rice is paired with 1 or more ingredients (like Fish or Meat)
and is wrapped around in seaweed (called Nori in Japanese).

Ingredients

  • 1 Can of Spam
  • Brown Sugar
  • Soy Sauce
  • Rice Vinegar
  • Spam Musubi Mold
  • Jasmine or Sushi Rice
  • Pack of Sushi Nori

Steps

  1. Prepare 12 cups of cooked rice (Roughly 4 Cups Uncooked Rice)
    • Optional: Mix in Rice Vinegar for Taste
  2. Open Sushi Nori Packet and Cut the Sheets in Half Horizontally
  3. Open Can of Spam and Cut in 1/4 inch Slices
  4. Use a non-stick Pan or Skillet on Medium Heat
  5. Mix Equal Parts Brown Sugar, Soy Sauce, and Rice Vinegar in a Bowl
    • This is the "Teriyaki Sauce"
  6. Dip Spam in Sauce and Cook 2 Minutes Each Side
  7. Cover a Plate with a Paper Towel and Lay Your Finished Spam There
  8. On a Cutting Board, Add Rice to the Spam Musubi Mold
    • Optional: Drizzle Teriyaki Sauce on Rice
  9. Dip Finished Spam into Sauce Again and Add to Musubi Mold
  10. Press the Mold and Wrap Finished Product in Nori
    • Use Water to Attach the End of the Nori to Itself
  11. Repeat and Enjoy!
